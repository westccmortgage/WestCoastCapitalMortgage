## Episode 7 — Income Type (`step-income-type.mp4`)

Now let's look at income type.

Not all income is evaluated the same way. Some borrowers earn W-2 income from
an employer. Others are self-employed, earn 1099 income, or own a business —
and each of those may call for different documentation and a different way of
calculating income.

That's why identifying the right income category early matters. The point isn't
just how much you earn; it's how that income may be evaluated for qualification.

Next, we'll look at qualifying income and how it can differ from gross income.

Questions about this step? A licensed mortgage professional can help you fully

## Episode 8 — Qualifying Income (`step-income.mp4`)

Let's talk about qualifying income.

A common misconception is that gross income and qualifying income are the same.
In reality, lenders evaluate income using underwriting guidelines — so things
like business expenses, deductions, rental income, and self-employment history
can all change the final qualifying number.

That's how two people earning similar amounts can qualify very differently. The
more accurately we evaluate income, the more accurate your financing strategy
becomes.

Next, we'll bring everything together and compare conforming and jumbo options.

Questions about this step? A licensed mortgage professional can help you fully
understand your situation.

## Episode 9 — Conforming vs. Jumbo (`step-result.mp4`)

This is one of the most important parts of the process.

Using the property location, loan amount, down payment, credit, and income, we
can compare financing paths. Some scenarios fit conforming or high-balance
conforming guidelines — loans at or under the county limit. Others call for
jumbo financing, which goes above that limit.

Neither is automatically better. Jumbo can have advantages in some situations;
conforming in others. The goal isn't simply to get a loan — it's to find the
most efficient financing structure for you.

Next, we'll look at how these choices affect your monthly payment.

Questions about this step? A licensed mortgage professional can help you fully
understand your situation.

## Episode 10 — Payment Strategy (`step-payment.mp4`)

Now let's look at payment strategy.

For many people, the monthly payment is the headline number — but it's best seen
as part of a bigger financial picture. Rate, loan term, down payment, and loan
structure all shape what you pay each month.

The lowest payment isn't always the best strategy, and the highest isn't always
necessary. The real aim is a payment that supports both qualification and your
long-term goals.

## Episode 11 — Buydowns & Points (`step-buydown.mp4`)

Let's talk about buydowns and points.

Some borrowers lower their interest rate by paying points at closing. Others use
a temporary buydown, which reduces payments in the early years of the loan. Both
can improve affordability and cash flow, depending on the situation.

The trade-off is cost — so we compare the upfront investment against the
long-term benefit. In some cases a buydown creates meaningful savings; in
others, keeping those funds available for other goals is the smarter move. It
depends on the borrower and the overall plan.

Next, we'll look at interest-only financing and when it may fit.

Questions about this step? A licensed mortgage professional can help you fully
understand your situation.

## Episode 12 — Interest-Only (`step-io.mp4`)

Now let's talk about interest-only financing.

With interest-only, you pay the interest without reducing the principal for a
set period. That can mean lower monthly payments and stronger cash flow, which
some borrowers find valuable.

It isn't the right fit for everyone, though, so it's worth understanding both the
benefits and the trade-offs. Used well, it's an effective planning tool; misread,
it can create unrealistic expectations — which is why we compare all the options
before deciding.

In our final step, we'll bring everything together and review the complete
strategy.
