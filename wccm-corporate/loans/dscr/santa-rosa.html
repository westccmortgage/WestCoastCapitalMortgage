Overview

What a DSCR loan means in Santa Rosa

DSCR stands for Debt-Service Coverage Ratio. A DSCR loan qualifies a Santa Rosa investment property based on whether its rental income covers the mortgage payment, rather than on your personal income. A DSCR of 1.0 means rent equals the payment; higher ratios indicate stronger cash flow. Santa Rosa's wine-country economy, county-seat employment, and steady demand support reliable rental occupancy, a balanced Sonoma County DSCR profile.

DSCR loans are non-conforming investor loans, so they are not capped by the conforming limit. Still, the 2026 one-unit conforming limit in Sonoma County is $897,000 (per FHFA/HUD 2026 loan limits), and the typical Santa Rosa home value is approximately $750K as of mid-2026 — useful benchmarks when you size a purchase.
