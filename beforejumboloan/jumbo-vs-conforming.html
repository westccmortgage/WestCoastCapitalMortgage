Jumbo vs. Conforming

Where the line sits in your county — and why staying “before jumbo” can change your options.

The three bands

Each county has a baseline conforming limit and, in high-cost areas, a higher high-balance conforming ceiling. Loans above that ceiling are reviewed as jumbo.

  • Conforming review — loan at or below the local baseline conforming limit.
  • High-balance conforming review — loan above baseline but at or below the county high-cost ceiling.
  • Jumbo review — loan above the county high-cost ceiling.
  • DSCR / rental-income review — investment scenarios reviewed on the property’s rent rather than personal income.
  • VA / FHA review — government-program review paths where eligible.
  • Self-employed / bank-statement review — alternative income documentation paths.

Why “before jumbo” matters

A different down payment or price can move a scenario from jumbo review into high-balance conforming review. The Strategy Studio shows that math as an illustration — jumbo may still be the right path for some buyers.
